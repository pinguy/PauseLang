The one thing still worth knowing

Loopback max jitter this run was 2.01ms against a ±1.5ms guard band — it exceeded the guard band on some individual intervals but the program still executed correctly because the quantizer snapped to the nearest valid opcode. 98.8% in-band means 1.2% of intervals are being decoded by nearest-neighbour rather than direct match. That's fine on loopback where opcodes are 5ms apart and noise is sub-3ms. On a real network hop where jitter could be 5–20ms, the cliff at ±3ms becomes a hard wall. The guard band width and the opcode spacing are the two parameters to tune for any new transport medium — everything else in the VM stays the same.
